Home Renovations Sydney Explained

A Paddington terrace under a conservation overlay, a Cronulla beachside kitchen and a Castle Hill second-storey addition are not the same project, even at similar square metreage. Renovation Sydney frames this as identifying the real renovation path early, whether that is whole-home, kitchen, bathroom, extension, granny flat, heritage or terrace work, before pricing gets discussed.

That early sorting matters because cost, approvals and finish quality are considered together rather than in isolation. A cosmetic refresh of a few rooms can sit in the tens of thousands, while a full whole-home renovation with structural change commonly runs from around $150,000 into the several hundred thousands. Materials are usually the largest single line item, with labour a significant share on top.

Kitchen and bathroom scope decisions

Kitchen and bathroom work make up most single-room renovation enquiries, and both scale on similar factors: cabinetry material, benchtop choice, and how much plumbing or electrical work is involved.

  • A budget kitchen refresh, new doors, benchtop and splashback with no layout change, often starts in the low-to-mid tens of thousands.
  • A mid-range full kitchen with new cabinetry, stone-look benchtops, appliances and services updates typically lands higher again.
  • Premium custom joinery with features like a butler's pantry and integrated appliances sits higher still.
  • Bathroom cost is driven by size, tiling extent, whether the layout moves, and fixture quality, with a same-layout renovation usually the more affordable path.

Waterproofing to Australian Standard AS 3740 is not optional in a compliant bathroom renovation; it is a fixed requirement regardless of budget tier.

Heritage terraces, harbourside homes and access constraints

Inner-city and harbourside Sydney carries renovation constraints that don't apply everywhere. Heritage terraces, harbourside homes, beachside units, project homes and apartments each bring different structural, access or council considerations into the same renovation type.

A terrace renovation in an area like Balmain or Paddington may face conservation overlay rules that a project-home renovation in Castle Hill never encounters. Narrow lane access, party walls and existing structural conditions can also change what a builder needs to price for, separate from the finish level you choose.

Approvals, written contracts and builder vetting

Council pathways, waterproofing requirements and structural work are meant to be considered upfront, before a firm price is set, rather than surfacing mid-build. That sequencing protects both the budget and the timeline.

Before work starts, a licensed builder should set out scope, price, programme and exclusions in writing. Builder credentials and insurance details are worth confirming at the same stage, alongside whether your project needs a development application, a complying development certificate, or falls under exempt works.

  1. Define the real project type. Work out whether the issue is whole-home, kitchen, bathroom, extension, granny flat, heritage or terrace work before requesting quotes.
  2. Flag heritage or structural factors early. Note any conservation overlay, harbourside setting, access constraint or structural change so builders can price accurately.
  3. Confirm compliance requirements. Check that any bathroom scope includes waterproofing to AS 3740 and that structural work accounts for council approval.
  4. Get a written, fixed-price quote. Request a licensed builder's written quote covering scope, price, programme and exclusions before agreeing to start.
  5. Sign a contract before work begins. Confirm the contract is in place, with credentials and insurance details checked, ahead of any site work starting.

Common questions

Does a heritage overlay always add cost to a renovation? Not automatically, but heritage terraces and conservation-overlay properties typically add approval steps and design constraints that a project-home renovation does not face, which can affect timeline and scope decisions.

Why is bathroom waterproofing non-negotiable? Waterproofing to Australian Standard AS 3740 is a compliance requirement for any renovated bathroom, regardless of whether the layout changes or the budget tier chosen.

What should a written renovation quote include? A compliant quote from a licensed builder should set out the scope, price, programme and exclusions in writing before work starts, so the contract reflects what was actually agreed.

Is a fixed quote the only reliable way to know renovation cost? Given how much scope, size and finish level affect price, a written fixed-price quote for your specific home remains the only reliable figure, rather than general cost ranges.
